Observations & Analysis

Sequential scanning processes one port at a time, waiting for each connection to timeout before moving to the next. With a 0.3s timeout, scanning 200 ports takes approximately 60.77s.

Concurrent scanning uses a thread pool to probe multiple ports simultaneously. With 100 threads, the same 200-port range completes in 0.71s — a 85.59x speedup.

Thread count scaling (Test 2) shows diminishing returns beyond ~200 threads, where OS scheduling overhead begins to offset the gains from parallelism.

Port range scaling (Test 3) shows near-linear growth in concurrent mode — doubling the range roughly doubles the time, while sequential mode degrades far more steeply due to serial timeouts accumulating.
